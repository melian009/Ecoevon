%Ecological and evolutionary processes are mixed together to drive population density and trait changes

%Most intuitive examples are biotic or abiotic factors driving trait and density changes within populations.

%Links between ecological and evolutonry processes have been incorporated in empirical and theoretical work for nearly a century.

%Yet, integrating rapid evolution or concurrent ecological and evolutionary time scales requires further research.

%In this intro we will discuss the importance of density- and frequency-dependent selection

%for understanding the connection between ecology and evolution

%----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

%One of the most fundamental theorems in evolution to understand trait change driven by selection is the Price equation

%---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

%The Price equation has two terms, the first describing the mean phenotypic change due to selection

%and the second transmission bias -- In ecological terms, selecting factors can be many,

%abiotic like climate or habitat conditions, and biotic like predators, parasites or others. The transmission bias

%change can be due to change in the environment, mutations, or other forces not explicitly introduced in the equation

%---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

%It can be represented (using LateX symbols) as

$$\Delta \overline{\phi} = \frac{1}{\overline{w}} (cov(w,\phi) + E(w \delta))$$

%LateX example$$e^x=\sum_{i=0}^\infty \frac{1}{i!}x^i$$
